Design Media Project 1 - Video Narrative Reconstruction Edit

For our first assessment we were tasked with creating an entirely new video out of two already made components; one would be a video we could use for visuals, and the other video can have its audio be reassembled to use as audio for our new video. The purpose of this task was to create an entirely new narrative using editing skills that we had learnt in class. This new video was to be assembled using the program Adobe Premiere Pro.

We were given a list of videos to choose from to create our reconstructed video edits from. I initially had chosen an animated documentary video about urban planning in the United Kingdom and made a rough edit from that without any audio. However after receiving feedback from my tutor and classmates about the edit, I decided to choose another video to focus my edit on.

Using a documentary video from the listed videos given to us titled "In Search of Ancient Astronauts", which focused on the theory of ancient astronauts having visited Earth before, and then using audio from a documentary titled "This is Marshall McLuhan: The Medium is the Message‚Äù, I creat an video in which the narrative focused on a group of people building a spaceship that visits the Moon and comes back to Earth. I also used stock music from YouTube Audio Library for the soundtrack of the short video.

Overall I was happy with the way the video ended up turning out in the end, and the editing skills that I had learned from this experience would not only come in handy for my time at university, but also for my future career skills as well.

Design Media Project 2 - Responsive Icon Design

Click the image above to interact with the icon designs!

In this assessment we were tasked with creating nine responsive icon designs, as well as a website to host the designs for users to interact with. The icon designs would all have to relate to an overall theme of our choosing and be created using Adobe Illustrator, with Visual Studio Code being used to build the coding of the website.

I first started off by using a sketching process to come up with various designs relating to a few different themes I had in mind for the assessment. After a while of experimenting with my designs and overall ideas I decided to focus on the theme of dreams; more specifically lucid dreaming. I choose this because of the fun possibilities that could come about due to the often surreal nature of lucid dreaming descriptions.

I began doing research on lucid dreaming and began brainstorming ideas on how to best present this overall theme in an effective manner for viewers to interact with. The most challenging aspect was how broad lucid dreaming as a whole can be for different people given the often abstract nature. However I used this abstract nature to my advantage, and create a overall narrative describing a persons experience during lucid dreaming from this. With the sound design I used a DAW (digital audio workstation) called Ableton Live to create the interactive sounds using synth plug-ins. Once this was all completed I then assembled an edited version of a template we were given in class for the website layout.

To conclude, I believe it was this assessment task in particular I began to notice my overall design aesthetic forming, which would influence the next assessment task heavily.

Design Media Project 3 - 3D Digital Environment

For this assessment we were tasked with creating a 3D digital environment walkthrough experience based on the theme of personal change faced by either someone we know or ourselves. We were also asked to showcase the change we or they had experience through the changing of landscape seen throughout the walkthrough experience. This experience was to be entirely created using the program Unity. We were allowed to use 3D assets that we sourced online to help us to create our own experiences.

After a lengthy thought and sketching process I decided to focus on my own personal change I've experienced in the year 2022; which was my move from Brisbane down to Melbourne, which also meant changing universities. I had somewhat grand ideas for how to showcase this in my 3D digital environment, but after a lengthy experimentation process using Unity I managed to narrow it down to a simple yet effective experience idea.

The digital environment implements a low poly cartoon-like aesthetic, which was inspired by various video games that I enjoy such as Katamari Demacy. I wanted to continue to use cartoon inspired aesthetics that I first implemented in my second assessment task, hoping to give a majority of my work in this class an overall sense of unity. Overall I was happy with how the final experience turned out, and through experimenting I managed to narrow down from a somewhat complicated initial idea to a simple and moving experience for users to witness for themselves.
